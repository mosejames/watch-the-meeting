College Park Is Selectively Silencing Council Meeting Recordings

We audited 132 public meeting recordings on the City of College Park's official YouTube channel.
18 have had their captions deliberately disabled. Zero budget meetings were affected.
The pattern is clear.

The Pattern

YouTube automatically generates captions on every uploaded video. It's a default feature. For captions to be missing, someone has to go into YouTube Studio and manually turn them off on each individual video.

We checked every meeting recording on the CollegeParkTV23 channel going back to mid-2024. Budget meetings have captions. Most workshops have captions. But specific council meetings, BIDA meetings, and special called meetings — the proceedings where votes happen, contracts are discussed, and confrontations take place — have had their captions deliberately removed.

Why This Matters

Accessibility. Deaf and hard-of-hearing residents are being locked out of their own government proceedings. YouTube auto-captions exist by default on every video. Someone had to go in and turn them off. This isn't an oversight. It's a choice to exclude.

Accountability. Without captions, these recordings can't be searched, quoted, or easily analyzed. The meetings where votes happen and deals get made become harder to hold anyone accountable for. Captions create a searchable text record. Removing them removes that record.

The Questions That Need Answers

Who is making this decision?

Captions don't turn themselves off. Someone with access to the CollegeParkTV23 YouTube Studio account is going into individual videos and disabling subtitles. Who authorized this? Who is doing it? And on whose direction?

What happened at these specific meetings?

The January 5 council meeting. The February 2 council meeting. The April 6 council meeting. The BIDA meetings where development deals are discussed. What was said in these recordings that someone doesn't want easily searchable or quotable?

Is this legal?

Government meetings are public record. The Americans with Disabilities Act requires reasonable accommodation for individuals with disabilities. Title II of the ADA applies to state and local governments. Selectively removing accessibility features from public government proceedings raises serious legal questions.

Why are budget meetings untouched?

Zero out of 13 budget meetings have been affected. Every category of meeting where political decisions, votes, and confrontations happen has been hit. The one category that's mostly spreadsheets and line items has been left completely alone. That's not random.

How We Verified This

We used YouTube's Transcript API to programmatically check every meeting recording on the CollegeParkTV23 channel dating back to mid-2024. A total of 132 recordings were checked, filtered to meetings 10 minutes or longer.

YouTube's API does not guess. When we query a video, it returns one of two distinct responses. There is no ambiguity between "captions don't exist yet" and "captions have been turned off." They are completely different responses from YouTube's system. Here is exactly what the API returns for a disabled video vs. an enabled video, side by side:

The TranscriptsDisabled exception is a specific error class in YouTube's system. It only fires when someone has gone into YouTube Studio and manually turned off subtitles on that video. It is not the same as "no transcript found" (captions never generated) or "video unavailable." YouTube is explicitly telling us: subtitles were turned off on this video.

Every video in the table above links directly to YouTube. You can verify any entry yourself by clicking the link and checking whether the CC (closed caption) button is available on the video player.

Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act requires state and local governments to provide accessible communications. Selectively disabling captions on public meeting recordings may violate federal law.

  • DOJ ADA Complaint Portal: ada.gov/file-a-complaint
  • HHS Office for Civil Rights: hhs.gov/ocr/complaints
