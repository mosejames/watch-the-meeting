BIDA erupts over $471K scandal — Vice chair survives removal vote in chaotic tie

Source: City of College Park Official YouTube
Meeting Briefing

The College Park BIDA meeting devolved into chaos as board members attempted to remove Vice Chair Tracy Wyatt over unauthorized broker agreements that cost the city $471,000. The heated 3-3 vote failed after bitter personal attacks and parliamentary confusion. Board member Jefferson's motion accused Wyatt of signing multiple unauthorized contracts with real estate brokers, including deals that implicated both BIDA and the City of College Park without proper approval. Mayor Brown defended following ethics procedures while other members demanded immediate action. The meeting exposed deep dysfunction, with members questioning each other's legal knowledge and trading accusations of convoluted information.
