$2.4 Million DMO Can't Answer Basic Questions

“We sit up here and we try we talk to the different vendors and the contractors that come through here... we're talking about millions of dollars here and we have no idea how it's being utilized.”

— Councilwoman McKenzie

College Park's destination marketing org takes millions but can't provide budgets, business lists, or World Cup plans.

Meeting Briefing

The March 25 workshop exposed stunning dysfunction at College Park's destination marketing organization, which collects $2.4 million annually but couldn't provide a budget for World Cup plans or basic business lists. Councilwoman McKenzie torched the DMO's Mercedes Miller for months of non-responsiveness while residents get nothing from FIFA 2026. Meanwhile, council debated financial guardrails requiring unanimous votes to touch reserves or issue bonds — a move that could paralyze emergency decisions. The meeting also featured a nonprofit director defending Ignite Resource Center's $50K funding request while admitting she doesn't track how many people she helps.
