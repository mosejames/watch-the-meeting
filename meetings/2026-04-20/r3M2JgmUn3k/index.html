McKenzie Explodes at Mayor Over 'Black Woman' Vendor Treatment

“I will never sit here and allow any person who has rolled up their sleeves, gives her their time... to be disrespected by the head of this meeting, the chair of this meeting.”

Councilwoman accuses mayor of 'continuously disrespecting' black women contractors and vendors

Meeting Briefing

What started as routine presentations turned into a public meltdown when Mayor Motley Broom aggressively questioned tourism contractor Karen Jeremy about a $24,000 bonus payment. Councilwoman McKenzie exploded, accusing the mayor of 'continuously disrespecting' black women vendors and moved to override the chair for being 'out of order.' The confrontation exposed deep personal animosity between McKenzie and the mayor, with McKenzie challenging the mayor to a face-to-face meeting to 'iron out differences.' Meanwhile, the city approved unbudgeted communication boards and entertained a $95,000 request for a 'love and reverence' program across all four wards.
